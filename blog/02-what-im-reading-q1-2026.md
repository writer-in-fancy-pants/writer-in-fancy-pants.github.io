I am trying to track what I read so I can revisit the snapshot when needed.

## Yoga
_Heart of Yoga_ — T K V Desikachar. One book that truly got me started on the path of understanding my yoga practice.

_Hatha Yoga Pradipika_ - Swami Muktibodhananda. Foundational text on Hatha Yoga practices. A traditional yoga reference manual. The theory meanders a bit across the sections, but instructions are detailed and quite useful.

_Raja Yoga_ — Swami Vivekananda. Short, succint book on the practice of Raja Yoga - controlling your own mind. Enlightening mostly, as long as you remember it was written in 1890s, before a lot of modern discoveries.

## Spirituality
_Freedom from the Known_ - J Krishnamurti. Absolutely blew my mind. Can you accept yourself, as you are? Not through any ideals, or lenses, but as you are. I was lucky enough to experience the state where thoughts seize, under unique circumstances, and feel so grateful to Krishnamurti for clearly describing what the state of fully aware blissful existence is. You can only leave the window open, so the breeze may come in.

_Be as you are_ - David Godman (Compiler), Sri Ramana Maharshi (Author). This book is a paradox for me. Every time I try to read it, I feel I am doing the opposite of what the author suggests - trying to find liberation, hence binding myself. Still reading it piecemeal.

_Tuesdays with Morrie_ - Mitch Albom. Finally read this book, 17 years after I first bought a copy. Nicely puts mortality in perspective, connecting Western and Eastern thought to an extent. Heartwarming. Great instructions to live by.

## Science / Pop Science
_The body keeps the score_ - Bessel van der Kolk. A monumental work that helps one understand mental health, and trauma. Due to my personal interest in helping friends deal with panic attacks, neurofeedback techniques, EMDR therapy, and understanding human behavior, I found this a most exciting read. 100\% recommended.

_Gödel, Escher, Bash : The Eternal Golden Braid_ - Douglas Hoffstadter. A book on strange loops, self-referential structures, paradoxes, models of thinking, formal systems, recursion, metacognition, zen, and more. I have started this book multiple times in different stages of my life, and never yet managed to finish. Still reading...
