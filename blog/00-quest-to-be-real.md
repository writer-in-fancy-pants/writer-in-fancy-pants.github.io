I have been on my journey for a while now. It is hard to really say when the journey began. There have been major pivots across my life - chronic illness, loss of fortune, restarting in a new country, intense spiritual retreats  - each could be the starting point with the past as prologue. Saying it began as a kid won't be wrong, since I am circling back to childhood memories and habits again after having lost them for so long. I find it is almost a necessary step in a journey. 


Yet, in a most practical ways, it all began 6 months ago, when I quit my job, with a lot of ideas and little certainty. Life has been a lot more chaotic emotionally, yet satisfying. Giving up on external structure that the 9-5 provided, I sometimes find myself adrift in the limbo of doing nothing. Other times, I am extremely engaged, working on 5+ projects in parallel, more plans in motion, and feeling fulfilled. It comes and goes.


During calls with multiple friends, I have been asked to make a record of what I'm up to, what I have discovered. I realize I want to be able to look back and have enough of the frame to be able to context switch into these mental states I'm living through. This website and blog, here, is a time capsule of myriad things - who I am now, really cool ideas and books, projects I'm working on, a record of my journey, a way to keep myself accountable, and share what I have found.

I don't know where I'll end up. But I do know I'm heeding the call, engaging with the world, and being more aware, more real.
