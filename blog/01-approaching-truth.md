"The truths contained in religious doctrines are after all so distorted and systematically disguised" - Sigmund Freud

The truth - of life, of nature, of the world, of human experience, of the universe - is a deeper interconnected web which seems indecipherable. Humanity has approached it from numerous directions, discovering ever evolving facets of something that seems so simple and yet seems to defy labels.

I grew up in a conservative Hindu family following vedic traditions, discovered the wonders of physics, found solace in fantasy, and earned living as a computer scientist. Most of all, I have learned from life with no particular mentor except those I could read of and from. Most of all, life itself has been kind to me, gifted with plethora of shocking incidents and a wide range of experiences.

Based on this, my approach to truth is narrowed to the following

- Read a lot
Books have this wonderful quality of being ever available, and being filled with long term efforts of an expert. It is easy to find something that many people resonate with, ie concepts close to the lived experiences of many people. Other mediums have this ability too. But written language, for all its flaws, forces both the author and the reader to have a conversation across time, using one's learning and imagination. The essence of books is timelessness. So read the books that stand the test of time. Read a good book, and then follow it up by other works of the same author to see where they are coming from. Read, and digest, read again once you have become someone else.

- Do nothing / Let yourself be (bored)
This one is hard for me. Our attention has become a commodity, one that is being hogged by everything surrounding us. The map that we created to understand the world has become a maze so convoluted that the world is hidden and we get lost wherever we look. In this time, it is important to be with yourself. Let yourself be bored, empty, not producing anything. Do things for the sake of doing them. Daydream. Let the thoughts wander. Experience the inner world. Experience the mundane world around you. Be in the moment. That is reality. The map is not the territory. Names and ideas and sensory perceptions are not the same as the very thing the describe. The world is interconnected, indescribable without a narrow objective. Once I turned off the spotlight from my preconceptions and plans for the world, only then did I see all the calls to adventure all around me.

- Long-term consistent practice
Humans are shortsighted in general. The pain of 1 child drowning is not very different from 1 million drowning. If a billion die 100 years from now from our one choice now, we can't even comprehend such a thing even if have the intellectual terminology for it. Long-term consistency has the ability to accomplish miracles, things that are inconceivable at the onset. Of course, methodology must advance with expertise. Learning must be active.. As TKV Desikachar said - our practice must be just a bit more clever than our habits.
